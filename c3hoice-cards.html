The C³ Choice Cards

Twelve navigation points for anyone standing at a fork. Tap any card to open it.

C³ = C : C → C
Circumstance · Choice · Consequence

Each card names one concept from the C³ framework. Together they form a complete map of how a human being chooses — and what makes that choosing wiser or poorer. Work through them in order, or go directly to the one you need most today.

Before You Begin
Card 01 · Entry Point
🗺
Your Choice History
You have always been choosing. Start by seeing the evidence.
Tap to open
Not everyone arrives at conscious choosing through a single dramatic moment. Some people need to look back first — to see the pattern of choices already made before they can own the ones ahead. How you have spent your time and money is the most honest mirror of your values available. The choice precedents forming quietly in your life are worth naming before you stand at the next fork.
What do your last five years of choices — time, money, direction — reveal about what you actually value?
Card 02 · The Mirror
🪞
The Revelatory Nature of Choice
Choices reveal values before they are consciously named.
Tap to open
Every choice is a self-portrait. You do not need to have named your values to have been living by them. Look at where your time and money have actually gone — not where you intended them to go. The gap between intention and actual behaviour is one of the most important things a chooser can examine honestly.
What is the gap between what you say you value and how you actually spend your time and money telling you right now?
The Framework
Card 03 · The Soil
🌐
The Field of Influence
The larger web of forces shaping what is even possible for you.
Tap to open
The Field of Influence is third-person and expansive. You did not choose your family, your culture, or the moment in history you arrived into. These are the situation cards on the table before you drew your first breath. The Field is not something you experience directly — it is the larger reality that shapes what circumstances are even possible. No choice is ever made in a vacuum.
What forces in your Field — cultural, relational, historical — are shaping what feels possible to you right now, without your full awareness?
Card 04 · C¹
🎨
Circumstance
The world as you experience it at the moment of choice.
Tap to open
Circumstance is first-person and situated. It is the specific conditions, pressures, possibilities, and constraints you face right now. Two people standing in the same room can have entirely different circumstances — because circumstance is not observed, it is lived. Your circumstances define the real array of options available to you on your canvas today.
What are the actual conditions — not the ideal ones — you are standing in right now? Name them honestly, including the ones that feel unfair.
Card 05 · The Compass
🧭
Truth & Convergence
Nobody holds a monopoly on truth — but you can point toward it.
Tap to open
Seek convergence, not certainty. Truth tends to appear where multiple streams agree — what feels right, what has practical utility, what holds up to scrutiny, what can be validated, and what connects with other things you know to be true. One stream alone, especially feeling, can mislead. Where they converge, you are closer to a reliable guide.
Which of the five tendencies of truth — feeling, convergence, utility, scrutiny, validation — is most alive in the decision you are facing right now?
Card 06 · The Inner Canvas
🔍
Knowing Your Self
Six aspects. One honest reckoning.
Tap to open
Alignment between a choice and your inner self is only possible if you know what your inner self actually is. The six aspects: your needs — what you require to flourish. Your beliefs — what you hold to be true. Your values — what matters when it costs something. Your interests — what sustains your attention. Your intentions — what you are actually trying to bring about. Your strengths — what you are genuinely capable of at your best.
Which of the six aspects of self is most out of alignment with the choice you are currently facing?
Card 07 · The Anchor
⚖️
Guiding Values
Name what matters before you name your options.
Tap to open
Before you map your options, name two or three values that should anchor this decision. Not what sounds good. Not what you wish you valued. What you actually value — evidenced by how you spend your time and money. Naming your guiding values before choosing makes the rating of pros and cons more deliberate and the alignment check at the end more honest.
What two or three things matter most to you in this specific decision — right now, given who you actually are today?
Card 08 · The Awakening
🌅
The Dawning of Agency
The moment you realise you are a chooser — not a passenger.
Tap to open
Agency is not a permanent state — it is a dawning. A moment of awakening to the fact that you can choose. That the problem you are facing is not happening to you — it is a fork you are standing at. The reframe changes everything. Not the circumstance. Your relationship to it. You are no longer a passenger. You are at the wheel.
Take what you are currently facing and reframe it. Not as a problem happening to you — as a point of decision you are standing at. What does that reframe reveal?
Card 09 · C²
⑂
Choice
The fork. The freedom. The full weight of it.
Tap to open
You sense you could have gone another way. That road not taken is real — when you pick one path, you genuinely forfeit the other. There is no gold choice. There are only choices that align — or do not — with who you are and who you are becoming. Not choosing is itself a choice, and it too has consequences. The spectrum runs from which toothpaste to buy to life-altering forks. Most of the choices that matter most are somewhere in between.
What are your three real options — not wishes, not fantasies — the genuine roads available to you right now? Name the one you have been avoiding naming.
Card 10 · The Circle
🤝
Wise Counsel
In an absurd world, connection is not weakness.
Tap to open
Your wise counsel are not outside your Field — they live within it. They expand your real array of options, help you read your circumstances more honestly, and shape better choices through the truth they are willing to tell you. Choosing your people is one of the highest-order choices you will ever make. A decision spoken aloud to an honest friend often becomes clearer — or reveals something you had not yet seen.
Who in your life is genuinely willing to tell you what you need to hear — not just what you want to hear? Have you shared this decision with them?
Card 11 · C³
🌊
Consequence
The inevitable echo. You do not direct it — you accept it.
Tap to open
Consequence is not something you control. It flows from your choice the way a ripple flows from a stone. You do not choose where the water goes. What you can control is this — whether you accept the consequences honestly, learn from them, and let them make you wiser. Accepted consequences become the lessons that strengthen your next choice. Denied consequences compound and find you later.
Looking at a past choice — one whose consequences you are still living — what would honest acceptance of those consequences teach you about the choice you are facing now?
Card 12 · The Living Loop
🪨
Your Choices Become Others' Circumstances
No choice is ever truly private.
Tap to open
Consequences flow back into the Field of Influence — and there they become new circumstances for other people. The Field shaped your Circumstance. Your Choice produced a Consequence. That Consequence re-enters the Field. And the Field becomes someone else's Circumstance — the ground from which their next choice is made. You are constantly editing someone else's canvas, whether you know it or not. Good choices expand the Field. Poor choices narrow it — often invisibly, often long after the original choice was made.
Whose circumstances will be shaped by the choice you are currently facing — and what do you owe them in how you choose?
The Sign-off
The Hopeful Humanist · The Choice Project
✦
May the choices you make be the wisest ones possible in the moments you meet them.
— Steve, The Hopeful Humanist
Tap to open
The goal is not a perfect choice. The goal is the wisest choice possible given who you are, what you know, and the moment you are standing in. That is enough. That has always been enough.
You have done the work. Whatever follows, this decision was made with honesty and intention. Own it fully.
